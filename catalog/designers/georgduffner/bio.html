Georg Mayr-Duffner is an Austrian type designer and open-source contributor based in Vienna. He is affiliated with WU Vienna University of Economics and Business, where he is associated with the university library.

He is best known for creating EB Garamond, an acclaimed open-source digitization of Claude Garamont's typeface designs based on the 1592 Egenolff-Berner specimen. The project aims to bring Renaissance typography into the digital age with support for extended Latin, Greek, and Cyrillic scripts. His work on EB Garamond has earned significant recognition in the open-source community.
