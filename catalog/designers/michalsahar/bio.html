Michal Sahar (born 1970) is an Israeli type designer, graphic designer, and typographer based in Tel Aviv. She runs her own design studio and is a senior lecturer and head of the Graphic-Typographic track in the Visual Communication Department at Bezalel Academy of Arts and Design, Jerusalem. She is a graduate of Bezalel and co-founder of Hagilda, a cooperative font foundry established in 2002 with Danny Mirav (Hatayas).

Sahar has designed branding and visual identities for major institutions including Bezalel Academy, Tel Aviv Museum of Art, Petach Tikva Museum, and Jaffa Port. She has received numerous awards including the Israeli Ministry of Culture Design Award (2004), ADC Bronze Cube (2012), Granshan 2nd prize for Fedra Hebrew (2015), and TDC Certificate of Typographic Excellence (2017).
