Ezer Type House is an independent type foundry based in Tel-Aviv. Established in 2001 by Oded Ezer, one of today's prominent Hebrew type designers, the foundry specializes in the creation of Hebrew and Latin typefaces for global clients such as Google, Samsung, Waze, and more. Ezer's typefaces have garnered national and international recognition, receiving Certificates of Excellence at the TDC52 competition hosted by the New York Type Directors Club and at the 'Bukva raz' type design competition in Moscow, Russia. Additionally, Ezer has been honored with the Israeli Education Ministry Prize for Design, among other accolades.
