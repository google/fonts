Sebastian Kosch is a type designer and software developer based in Vancouver. He is best known for creating Crimson, an open-source serif typeface family released in 2010, when high-quality open-source text fonts were limited.

The Crimson typeface draws inspiration from classical typography, particularly the work of designers like Jan Tschichold and Robert Slimbach. In 2018, Crimson Pro was released as a professionally redesigned version by Jacques Le Bailly, commissioned by Google.
