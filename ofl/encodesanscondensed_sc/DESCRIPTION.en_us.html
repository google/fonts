Encode Sans is a versatile workhorse sans-serif design, featuring a huge range of weights and widths, ready for all kind of typographic challenges.

This is a Smallcap version, made to allow use of the OpenType smallcap feature within word processors and to provide a smaller font file for contexts in which only smallcaps are desired. You can find other widths at Normal SC, SemiCondensed SC, SemiExpanded SC, and Expanded SC.

Additionally, Encode Sans includes a full Condensed family, which is part of the superfamily along with Condensed, SemiCondensed, SemiExpanded, and Expanded families, each with 9 weights.

This project was started by Impallari Type, a foundry based in Rosario, Argentina. It was initially designed by Pablo Impallari and Andres Torresi, and later refined by Jacques Le Bailly and others. To contribute, see github.com/thundernixon/Encode-Sans.
