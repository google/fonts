Gelasio is metrics compatible with Georgia in its Regular, Bold, Italic and Bold Italic weights.

Because of the very robust features found in Gelasio's design it is ideally used at in smaller sizes and for longer texts.

Gelasio feels generally formal and rational but offers an occasional flourish. This is more common in the italics. Gelasio was modeled after an original printed sample of a French Transitional or Reale type belonging to the author. Transitional types are marked by and interest in rational planning. As a result they tend to feature a tension between rigor and expression. This style of type traces its origin to the Romain Du Roi typeface which was introduced in 1702.
