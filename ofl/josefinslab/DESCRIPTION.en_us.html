Although he started simple with Josefin Sans, Santiago Orozco likes to think Josefin Slab was the first typeface he designed. Following the 1930s trend for geometric typefaces, he was inspired to create something between Kabel and Memphis with modern details.

He wanted to stick to the idea of Scandinavian style, so he put a lot of attention to the diacritics, especially to "æ" which has loops connecting in a continuous way, so the "e" slope was determined by this character. It also has some typewriter style attributes, because he’s liked the Letter Gothic typeface since he was in high school.
