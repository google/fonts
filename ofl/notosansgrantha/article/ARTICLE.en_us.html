Noto Sans Grantha is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the Indic Grantha script.

Noto Sans Grantha contains 478 glyphs, 24 OpenType features, and supports 121 characters from 3 Unicode blocks: Grantha, Vedic Extensions, Devanagari.

Supported writing systems

Grantha

Grantha (𑌗𑍍𑌰𑌨𑍍𑌥) is an Indic abugida, written left-to-right. Used since the 7th century CE for writing religious texts in Sanskrit and Dravidian languages. Related to Tamil. Needs software support for complex text layout (shaping). Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
