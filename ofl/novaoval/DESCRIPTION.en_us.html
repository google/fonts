Wojciech Kalinowski created the NovaCut font about 14-15 years ago for making inscriptions on stone. Initially the font contained only capitals and digits and existed only on paper and stone inscriptions. In 2010 he transferred this font to a computer, and made the missing lowercase and some basical signs; it was initially named Gothica..

He made other versions of some letters and created new fonts based on these letters. This resulted in seven fonts making up the Nova family. (NovaCut, NovaFlat, NovaOval, NovaRound, NovaSlim, NovaScript, NovaSquare - by the shape of individual fonts) and an eighth monospace font â€“ NovaMono.
