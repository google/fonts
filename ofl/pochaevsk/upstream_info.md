## Font Description

Pochaevsk is a contemporary Church Slavonic display font designed by Aleksandr Andreev. It was added to Google Fonts on 2024-12-06. The typeface reproduces the style of editions published by the Holy Dormition Pochaiv Lavra in the late 19th century and subsequently by Holy Trinity Monastery in Jordanville, New York, in the 20th century.

A distinguishing characteristic of Pochaevsk is its small vertical metrics, which made it convenient for bilingual editions featuring Church Slavonic text alongside text in other languages. The font targeted the Church Slavonic liturgical language (`cu_Cyrl`) as its primary language.
