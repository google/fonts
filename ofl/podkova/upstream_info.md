## Font Description

Podkova (Russian for "horseshoe") is a monoline slab serif typeface initially designed by Ilya Yudin in 2010 and carefully refined and expanded by Alexei Vanyashin in January 2017. In September 2019, the family was converted to a variable font. The wide proportions and clean features aided legibility at small sizes, while the unusual letterforms provided enough character for display typography.

The font was first added to Google Fonts on 2011-05-18, making it one of the earliest entries in the catalog. The variable font covered a weight range from Regular (400) to ExtraBold (800).
