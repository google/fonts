## Font Description

Półtawski Nowy is a digitization of the Antykwa Półtawskiego typeface, a key Polish typographic design created by Adam Półtawski in 1928. The digital version was designed by Mateusz Machalski, Borys Kosmynka, and Ania Wieluńska, and was added to Google Fonts on 2023-03-10.

The digitization drew on scans of a type specimen book from the Idźkowski i S-ka foundry and a set of drawings, punch patterns, and punches made by Monotype in Salfords, Great Britain between 1934 and 1955, made available by the Type Archive London. The project was implemented with support from the Digital Culture 2020 program of the Polish Ministry of Culture and National Heritage.

The digital version standardized characters and balanced typographic rhythm while retaining the ornamental character of the original, and extended the basic character set with small caps, subscript/superscript, multiple numeral variants (tabular, proportional, nautical), and a comprehensive set of OpenType features. The family was intended for typesetting in sizes from 10 to 18 pt.

The `display_name` field in the metadata used the Polish character: "Półtawski Nowy".
