## Font Description

Poppins is a geometric sans serif typeface developed by Indian Type Foundry (ITF). It was added to Google Fonts on 2015-06-03. The family supported both the Devanagari and Latin writing systems, making it an internationalist design within the geometric sans genre.

The Devanagari design, initially designed by Ninad Kale, was the first ever Devanagari typeface with a range of weights in the geometric sans genre when first published in 2015. The Latin was initially designed by Jonny Pinhorn. Both scripts were based on pure geometry, particularly circles, with each letterform nearly monolinear and optical corrections applied at stroke joints to maintain even typographic color.

The family was expanded over time by the ITF studio team to include nine weights (Thin through Black) in both upright and italic styles, totaling 18 static font files.

## Scripts and Subsets

The font supported Devanagari, Latin, and Latin Extended character sets. The primary script was Devanagari (`Deva`).
