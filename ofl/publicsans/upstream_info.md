## Font Description

Public Sans is a strong, neutral sans serif typeface developed by the United States Web Design System (USWDS), the official design framework for U.S. federal government websites. It was added to Google Fonts on 2019-06-07. The typeface was derived from Libre Franklin and was designed as a neutral, highly legible face for interfaces, body text, and headings.

The family was upgraded to variable fonts in May 2022, enabling a single file to cover the full weight range from Thin (100) to Black (900). A minisite was maintained at https://public-sans.digital.gov/.

The designers credited in the metadata included USWDS, Dan Williams, Pablo Impallari, and Rodrigo Fuenzalida — reflecting both the original Libre Franklin contributors and the USWDS development team.
