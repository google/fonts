## Summary

Sixtyfour Convergence was designed by Simon Cozens and Jens Kutílek as an extension of the Sixtyfour family from the homecomputer-fonts project. It added two additional axes — XELA (X Elasticity, -100 to 100) and YELA (Y Elasticity, -100 to 100) — on top of the existing BLED (0 to 100) and SCAN (-53 to 100) axes, enabling perspective-like distortion effects. It supported Latin, Latin Extended, Math, and Symbols subsets. The family was accompanied by an article documenting its design, included as HTML and images in the repository.
